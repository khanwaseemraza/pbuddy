# Optional Parcel Cover

Parcel cover on PBuddy is **optional**. You choose whether to add it when you
fund a booking. This page explains, in plain terms, what it is and what it is not.

## What it is

- An **optional** protection a Sender can add to a parcel, for an additional
  amount shown clearly before you pay.
- Provided by an **authorised insurer or intermediary** under that provider's own
  policy terms, limits, excess, and exclusions.
- A way to get help toward the value of an item if it is lost or damaged in
  carriage, subject to those terms.

## What it is not

- It is **not** provided or underwritten by PBuddy. PBuddy is not your insurer.
- It is **not** automatic — if you do not add it, the parcel is not covered by it.
- It does **not** make a parcel "fully insured", and PBuddy never describes it
  that way. Cover is always subject to limits and exclusions.
- It does **not** cover prohibited items, undeclared contents, or an inaccurately
  declared value.

## How a claim works

If a covered parcel is lost or damaged, raise it in the app. The claim is
assessed under the insurer's terms. You may need to provide evidence of the
item's value and what happened. An excess may apply.

## Cost-sharing is unaffected

Optional cover is a separate, clearly itemised charge. It does not change the
cost-sharing limit on the Buddy's contribution.

> **Note for review:** before this toggle is enabled in production, PBuddy must
> confirm the distribution arrangement with the chosen authorised insurer (for
> example, as an Introducer Appointed Representative) so that offering this cover
> is compliant under FSMA 2000.
