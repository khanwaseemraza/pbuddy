# Green Claims Methodology

PBuddy makes **no** environmental claim until it passes this methodology. The
standard is the CMA **Green Claims Code**, the greenwashing provisions of the
**Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024 (DMCCA)**, and the ASA/CAP
advertising rules. This document is the gate: a claim ships only when its row in
the evidence table below is complete and signed off.

## The six tests every claim must pass (CMA Green Claims Code)

1. **Truthful and accurate** — the claim is correct and we can prove it.
2. **Clear and unambiguous** — plain meaning; no implied wider benefit.
3. **Not omitting or hiding** material information.
4. **Fair comparisons** — like-for-like, against a clearly stated baseline.
5. **Considers the full life cycle** — we do not cherry-pick one favourable stage.
6. **Substantiated** — backed by robust, up-to-date evidence held on file.

## PBuddy-specific rules

- **Always name the baseline.** A comparative claim (e.g. "lower-carbon than X")
  must state *than what* — e.g. "compared with a dedicated van making the same
  single trip". Never a bare "green" / "eco" / "sustainable".
- **No absolute claims** ("zero-carbon", "carbon-neutral", "climate-positive")
  unless independently verified and evidenced; default to relative, evidenced
  claims.
- **Only count genuine marginal impact.** Our case is that the Buddy is *already*
  making the journey, so the marginal carbon of carrying a parcel is low. The
  comparison and the wording must reflect *marginal* impact, not pretend the
  journey itself is free.
- **No offset-based "neutral" claims** without disclosing the offset scheme and
  its limitations.
- **Keep the evidence.** Each live claim has a dated evidence file and a named
  owner; claims are reviewed at least annually or when the basis changes.
